How U.S. law is layered
When two laws conflict, the higher layer wins (the Constitution's “Supremacy Clause”). Federal beats state beats local — but only where the federal government has power to act.
The two court systems — and how a case moves
The U.S. runs two parallel court systems — federal and state. Most cases stay in one, climbing by appeal. Only federal questions can reach the U.S. Supreme Court from a state.
U.S. Supreme CourtThe final word — takes a tiny, chosen few cases from the top of either system (a “writ of certiorari”).
