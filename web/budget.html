How a shutdown happens
1

Congress must pass 12 appropriations bills each year to fund the government, and the President must sign them.

2

If they can't agree in time, they can pass a continuing resolution (CR) — a stopgap that keeps funding at current levels for a while.

3

If neither a full bill nor a CR is in place when funding runs out, agencies lose spending authority — a government shutdown.

4

During a shutdown, "non-essential" operations pause and many federal workers are furloughed until funding is restored.
